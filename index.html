Amazon Web Services ensures that more than 300,000 databases from Oracle or Microsoft have migrated to its cloud platform.

Amazon Web Services ensures that more than 300,000 databases from Oracle or Microsoft (two of its main competitors) have migrated to its cloud platform and that, for this, they have used the AWS Database Migration Service. Among these migrations, AWS highlights the one carried out by Samsung Electronics, which moved its more than 1.1 billion users on three continents from Oracle to Amazon Aurora. According to AWS, this has reduced their monthly costs by 44%.

An internal migration

AWS also explains that they have experienced on their own meat what it means to make a migration of this type. Its consumer division, in fact, also used Amazon DMS in conjunction with the AWS schema conversion tool to migrate more than 75 petabytes of data from 7,500 Oracle databases to multiple AWS databases including Aurora, DynamoDB, Amazon Relational Database Service and Amazon Redshift.

From your house to the cloud

Oracle ha tenido en las bases de datos buena parte de su negocio tradicional. Microsoft también desarrolló SQL para poder gestionar estas bases de datos. Son uno de los recursos más críticos de cualquier compañía, puesto que la información que almacenan es utilizada por otras muchas aplicaciones, como ERP o CRM. Son muchos los jugadores del mercado de cloud computing que ofrecen opciones para que los clientes migren sus instalaciones tradicionales, instaladas en sus propios servidores (on-premise) hacia la nube. Buena parte de la contienda comercial entre estas grandes proveedores (como AWS, Microsoft u Oracle) está precisamente en quedarse con estos saltos a la nube de los clientes que tienen estos recursos en on-premise.

'Anon': a cult sci-fi piece from 'Gattaca' director about the value of anonymity and privacy comes to Amazon Prime.

It comes to Amazon Prime Video, almost without making noise and after a season in the Movistar + and Filmin catalog, where you can also find it, the movie that Andrew Niccol shot in 2018 and which was released almost all over the world through Netflix ( He was part of the platform's fake 'Netflix Original' team). This erratic distribution and some misunderstanding on the part of the critics prevented it from reaching the box office further and over the months it has become an almost secret allegorical science-fiction piece destined for the most coffee growers of the genre. Niccol took the world of film science fiction by storm in the late 1990s with two flawless films, now considered modern classics: 'Gattaca' and the screenplay for 'The Truman Show', directed by Peter Weir. Time has turned them into essential pieces of the genre thanks to their radically humanist perspective, far from the typical and cynical cyberpunk approaches, and also thanks to distancing themselves from the spectacularized vision of the genre that space opera represents a la 'Star Wars', posing highly recognizable allegories about the direction in which a society enslaved by the abuse of technology is going.

The European Union plans a new strategy to curb the power of the Internet giants

The European Union is working on new measures that seek to curb, once again, the market power of large Internet companies. According to information provided by sources related to Brussels regulators to the Financial Times, a list of 20 companies is being created that would have to follow stricter standards than those imposed for the market in general. How would the 20 firms be selected for this list? According to the information, criteria are being defined such as the company's income, the number of users or that the brand has an essential tool that other companies need to use. Therefore, it is believed that Apple, Google, Amazon and Facebook could be some of those that meet the requirements to be on this list. These 20 brands, according to the leaked information, would have to follow a more rigorous regulation that is yet to be known. Two of the rules would force companies to be more transparent and share information with smaller companies. It remains to be seen how such concepts will be put into practice.

Amazon Web Services Joins Cohesity in DMaaS Industry to Offer AWS Cloud Data Management

Cohesity has just announced the signing of a strategic collaboration with Amazon Web Services (AWS) to launch its offering in the Data Management as a Service (DMaaS or Data Management as a Service) sector. The agreement means that these services are managed by Cohesity and hosted in the AWS cloud and are intended for medium-sized companies. In conjunction with this announcement, Amazon has made an equity investment in Cohesity, although financial terms are not disclosed. The firm also has DFJ Growth, Foundation Capital, Sequoia Capital and SoftBank as investors. These advertised services include data archiving and backup, disaster recovery, data copy management, and analytics. The foundation of everything is Cohesity Helios, a data platform based on SaaS (Software as a Service) that currently serves more than 1,500 customers.

  • Centralized data management services:Within the DMaaS solution, customers will be able to subscribe to various data management services but all through the same provider, which will be Cohesity. On many occasions, companies turn to various providers (one for backup, another for disaster recovery, others for data copy management and security ...). In this case, only one program must be used, which makes the tool easier.
  • Advanced security and ransomware detection:Helios currently analyzes more than 400,000 events daily and uses machine learning to detect anomalies that could indicate an ongoing ransomware attack. Clients of these DMaaS services can use these capabilities to detect, respond to, and recover from such an attack.
  • Simplified data management: customers will have the ability to manage their existing environments on premises and in the cloud.
  • "Pay to play": Prices will be based on the predictability of the consumption of the services.
  • Services supported by AWS: Users can also take advantage of cloud-based services provided by Amazon Web Services, such as Amazon Macie, a data security and privacy service that uses machine learning to protect sensitive data; and Amazon SageMaker, which gives data scientists and developers the ability to quickly create, train, and deploy machine learning models.
